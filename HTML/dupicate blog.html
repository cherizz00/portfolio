Mastering Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA) is essential for cracking tech interviews in 2025. We've evaluated the leading DSA courses in India, focusing on features like live classes, mentorship, interview prep, and student outcomes. Our list ranks LogicMojo's DSA course at #1, followed by other popular programs (Scaler, Coding Ninjas, GeeksforGeeks, etc.) trusted by Indian learners. Below, find a detailed ranking, a comparison table, and an FAQ section to help you choose the right DSA program.

Ranking Criteria

We assessed each course based on: comprehensive curriculum (core & advanced DSA topics), mode of instruction (live vs. self-paced), mentorship (1:1 doubt sessions, career guidance), hands-on projects, placement support (mock interviews, resume help), student reviews/success stories, and overall value (cost vs. benefits).

1. LogicMojo – DSA + System Design (Live Course)

LogicMojo's flagship DSA course (7-month live program) tops our list for its depth and support. It covers DSA from basics to advanced, plus system design (HLD/LLD). Classes are live on weekends (3h each), with 1:1 mentorship and unlimited doubt sessions. Students work on real projects and get continuous feedback; one review highlights "assignments after every session" and "interactive live doubt clearance" to reinforce learning. The course fee is around ₹45,000 (with EMI options). LogicMojo claims high placement success – e.g. students landing 80 LPA offers.

Pros:

  • Expert mentors, extensive live support, lifetime access
  • Mock interviews and resume guidance
  • Students report cracking top-company interviews (e.g. Cisco, Amazon) during or shortly after the course

Cons:

  • Long duration and premium price
  • Requires consistent commitment

2. Scaler Academy (by InterviewBit) – DSA & Algorithms Program

Scaler (InterviewBit Academy) offers a rigorous DSA program geared to product-job interviews. Its industry-vetted curriculum includes fundamentals, advanced DSA (4.5 months), plus modules on SQL, system design and even GenAI. Courses are live and include 1:1 mentorship from veteran engineers, real-world projects, and an "aspirational network" of peers. Career support is strong – mock interviews, resume/profile building, and referrals are built into the program. Scaler's programs typically span ~10–12 months. Pricing is high (often ₹3–4 lakh for premium tracks), though scholarships/EMIs are available.

Pros:

  • Highly structured content, expert faculty
  • Intensive practice, strong placement network

Cons:

  • Very costly and competitive to get into
  • Requires serious dedication

3. Coding Ninjas – DSA (CareerCamp & Premium Courses)

Coding Ninjas is well-known for placement-oriented courses. Their CareerCamp DSA (in collaboration with IIT Madras) is a 4-month live program. It includes 10+ mock interviews, resume reviews, profile building and access to a dedicated placement cell. Instructors cover core DSA topics plus contest programming. The total fee for such courses starts around ₹19,999. Coding Ninjas also offers longer "Ninja Career Camp" programs (6–8 months, ~₹30k–₹40k) with additional mentorship.

Pros:

  • Structured curriculum co-designed with IIT faculty
  • Active career services (1:1 mock interviews, placement support)
  • Lots of practice problems and peer coding contests

Cons:

  • Mostly targeted at college students; less hand-holding than full bootcamps
  • Medium range price (still an investment)

4. GeeksforGeeks – DSA Self-Paced Course

GeeksforGeeks (GfG) offers a self-paced DSA course that's hugely popular in India (100K+ students). It provides 60+ hours of recorded lectures (by GfG founder Sandeep Jain), 200+ practice problems, 10 coding contests, and 5+ DSA projects. Learners get 12 months of doubt support and multiple-choice quizzes. Topics cover everything from arrays and trees to advanced DP and segment trees. All this comes at an affordable price – roughly ₹3,899 (with optional ₹1,499 for 24×7 doubt resolution).

Pros:

  • Extremely affordable and flexible
  • Comprehensive content with certificate
  • Good for beginners and interview prep

Cons:

  • 100% self-study (no live classes)
  • Less mentor interaction and no personalized support (beyond forum/doubt team)

5. Coding Blocks – Master DSA (C++ & System Design)

Coding Blocks' DSA course (4–6 months) is thorough, especially in C++. It covers "foundation to advanced" topics with ~350+ coding problems and 6+ live projects. They offer both self-paced ("Lite") and live ("Premium") options. Courses begin at about ₹1,500 for basic tiers (premium versions go higher). Features include certificate of completion, personalized feedback, and optional placement assistance.

Pros:

  • Well-rounded syllabus with system design (HLD/LLD) integrated
  • Flexible learning modes (classroom/live/online)
  • Many practice problems

Cons:

  • Focus is on C++ (though concepts transfer)
  • Less brand recognition than bigger players

6. PrepBytes – Master DSA Program

PrepBytes' Master DSA program is a 3-month, ₹15,999 course that blends recorded and live sessions. It features 25+ hours of video lectures and 15+ live coding sessions for doubt-solving. The course promises a personalized program (adaptive pacing) and mentorship from industry experts. Students solve 150+ curated coding problems and take quizzes after each topic.

Pros:

  • Smaller batches for personalized attention
  • Frequent live doubt sessions
  • Certificate, lifetime access

Cons:

  • Newer brand – fewer alumni reviews
  • Limited to a 3-month duration (intensive learning needed)

7. Rahul Shetty Academy – Udemy DSA Courses

On Udemy, instructor Rahul Shetty offers top-rated self-paced DSA courses (often titled "Master DSA – Crack Coding Interviews"). These video courses use whiteboard teaching to cover topics like recursion, trees, graphs, heaps, and hashing. They include "tons of assignments & interview questions with solutions" for practice. Pricing is very low (Udemy coupons often bring it under ₹1,000).

Pros:

  • Extremely affordable, lifetime access
  • Beginner-friendly explanations
  • Good for brushing up independently

Cons:

  • No live interaction or real-time mentorship
  • Quality depends on learner's self-discipline

8. Edureka – DSA in Java (Live Internship Program)

Edureka offers a "Data Structures & Algorithms in Java" internship program (4 months, live instructor-led). It emphasizes "strong DSA knowledge" with hands-on projects. The platform touts an 80%+ completion rate and "lifetime access" to content. They also highlight partnerships with IITs/NITs for course design and provide 24×7 live support. While the exact fee varies (often around ₹30k–₹40k for premium bootcamps), Edureka frequently runs discounts.

Pros:

  • Interactive live classes, periodic assignments
  • Close to corporate trends
  • Good brand and certificate

Cons:

  • Batch pace may be fast for some
  • Relatively high cost compared to self-paced options

9. Unacademy (DSA Live Classes)

Unacademy (a large Indian ed-tech platform) offers Comprehensive DSA courses taught by popular educators like Love Babbar. These are live streaming classes (usually free with subscription) covering topics from basics to advanced algorithms. They include homework and doubt-clearing sessions. Since these courses are part of Unacademy's subscription model, pricing is via a monthly/yearly plan.

Pros:

  • Engaging lectures by well-known coaches
  • Integrated practice
  • Easy access (mobile app/web)

Cons:

  • Not a fixed curriculum – coverage can vary
  • Less formal structure and certification than bootcamps

10. Free IIT/NPTEL DSA Courses

For self-motivated learners, there are free courses by Indian institutes. For example, NPTEL/IIT offers online DSA courses (often on edX/YouTube) taught by IIT professors. These cover core algorithms theory and include assignments/quizzes. They are 8–12 weeks long and you can audit for free (certificate usually costs a small fee).

Pros:

  • High-quality academic content at no cost
  • Great for fundamentals and strong computer science grounding

Cons:

  • No live guidance or interview focus
  • Self-paced learning requires discipline

Frequently Asked Questions

Which DSA course is best in India?

It depends on your goals and budget. We've ranked LogicMojo's live DSA + System Design course as #1 for its comprehensive curriculum and mentoring. Other top choices include Scaler Academy (for an intensive, mentor-led program) and Coding Ninjas (for shorter, career-focused bootcamps). GeeksforGeeks and Udemy courses are great value options. Ultimately, the "best" course aligns with how much live support, interview prep, and study time you need.

How much time does it take to learn DSA?

This varies by course and learning pace. Many bootcamps are 3–7 months long (e.g. LogicMojo is 7 months, Coding Ninjas' CareerCamp is ~4 months, GfG self-paced is 8–16 weeks). A motivated learner practicing daily can achieve solid DSA proficiency in 3–6 months. For full coverage (including advanced topics), plan on several months of consistent study.

Do these courses guarantee placement or jobs?

No course can guarantee a job, but many offer robust interview preparation and placement support. For example, LogicMojo includes Amazon-level mock interviews and referrals, and Coding Ninjas provides mock interviews and resume help. The value lies in the training and practice they provide – success still depends on your own effort and performance in real interviews.

How should I choose the right DSA course?

Consider your learning style, schedule, and budget. If you need live guidance and accountability, a mentor-led program (LogicMojo, Scaler, Edureka, etc.) is ideal. If you're self-motivated, affordable self-paced courses (GfG, Udemy) work well. Look at curriculum depth, demo class availability, student reviews, and extra perks (like 1:1 mentoring or career cells) before deciding.

How much does a top DSA course cost?

Prices vary widely. Affordable self-study courses run ₹500–₹4,000 (like GfG, Udemy). Mid-tier bootcamps (Coding Ninjas, Coding Blocks, PrepBytes) range ~₹10k–₹20k. Premium programs with live mentorship (LogicMojo, Scaler, Edureka) can be ₹30k to ₹300k+. Check EMI options and scholarship tests (e.g. Coding Blocks CAST) to manage costs.

Are free resources enough to learn DSA?

You can learn basics for free (YouTube channels, NPTEL, GfG practice). However, paid courses often provide structure, mentorship, and interview coaching. Many learners combine resources – start with free tutorials and then enroll in a paid bootcamp for personalized guidance and mock interviews.

By considering the above options and FAQs, you can pick a DSA course that fits your needs. All the courses listed here have helped Indian learners prepare for coding interviews – evaluate their features, sample content, and success stories to make the best choice.
